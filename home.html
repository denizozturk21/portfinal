BRYTE Website redesign

reimagining tappity

Brown Refugee Youth Tutoring and Enrichment(BRYTE) is the most populated club at Brown with over 190 people tutoring and same number of people getting tutored. During the pandemic the club needed more internet outreach. Therefore, I decided to redesign BRYTE's website and address their usability and accessibility issues on their old website such as images missing alternate text, not following text hierarchy, accessibility for lower bandwidths, and simplifying the information hierarchy. The website received over 2000 visits from 14 different countries after less than a week, and BRYTE is still receiving applications without activity fairs.

For this project, we had to choose a startup and design a user interface (UI) for that startup without looking at their existing UI. The only thing we could work off of was their concept. The startup we chose is Tappity, an app for kids which teaches 100+ science topics with experiments and games. The app is specifically for children aged 4-10 and explores topics, such as space, animals, the human body etc. We did Lo-Fi and Hi-Fi mockups and did user testing to evaluate our design. In order to see our full interface and user testing process click on our write-up below.

Religious text generation

This is a hypothetical start-up front-end I made. Go-Dutch is a website where you can browse cars that are not used by their owners for a period of time, and you can lease a variety of secondhand cars while paying a small shipping cost while we switch your car within the same lease period.  Respectively, you have your filtering options, a catalog of cars where you can save cars you want into your cart, and a section where you can see what cars are in your cart along with costs/discounts and checkout option. The name Go-Dutch comes from the expression going Dutch which means splitting the cost equally. Go-Dutch allows users to lease their dream cars by going Dutch on the lease with others month by month. This was overall a great topic to learn React Hooks for e-commerce.

Text generation of religious texts with LSTM models was a Deep Learning project I had that was controversial as a topic. This model is trained on a cleaned dataset of the Quran and the Bible, and generates single verses. Religious text is the only form of information that is assumed to be true without any concrete evidence. So I wanted this to be a model that can potentially pass the Turing test in a different way: can a machine convince others that the text is words of God? This project is dedicated to create a model to be able to test this, without having performed a formal test yet.
